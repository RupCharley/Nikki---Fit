🚴 Recumbent Bike
An excellent low-impact option — particularly good on days when energy is low, or as a substitute for the stepper. The seated position is very comfortable and pelvic-floor friendly.
Session A Substitute (25 min)
If the stepper feels too demanding or your knees need a rest, swap to the recumbent bike. Use moderate resistance and keep a steady pace — you'll get equivalent cardio benefit with zero impact.
Machine Busy Substitute (20 min)
If the rowing machine or elliptical is occupied on Session B, the recumbent bike is the best alternative. No waiting — just get on and keep moving.
⚡ Vibrating Plate
Not a cardio machine — but a useful recovery and activation tool. Best used after your session, before or after the sauna.
Post-session (8–10 min)
Stand on the plate after your session — feet hip-width, knees softly bent. Stimulates circulation, helps flush lactic acid, and reduces next-day soreness. Can hold a squat or calf raise position for added benefit.
Positions to try
· Standing — basic recovery, circulation
· Soft squat hold — activates glutes and quads during vibration
· Calf raises — great for lower leg recovery
· Hands on plate, plank — core activation (if comfortable)
⚠️ Avoid if you have any joint pain or dizziness. Start with the lowest setting and increase gradually. Not a substitute for cardio — it complements recovery.

Protocol: Always use after gym sessions, never before. Drink 500ml water before entering. Stop immediately if lightheaded. 3–4 sessions/week optimal for recovery and sleep.

Cardio — Stepper
CARDIO
Stepper Machine
▾
💡 The stepper is particularly good for lifting the glutes and toning the backs of the legs — exactly the muscles that benefit most.
How to do it
1
Stand tall — resist the urge to lean forward or hunch
2
Hold the rails very lightly — fingertips only, not supporting your weight
3
Drive each step from your glute and the back of your thigh — feel it working
4
Full range of motion on each step — don't take short choppy steps
5
Keep a steady rhythm, breathing comfortably
Common mistakes
✗
Leaning heavily on the rails — this removes all the benefit
✗
Short choppy steps — use the full range the machine allows
✗
Looking down — keep your head up and chest open
✗
Going so fast you lose form — moderate pace with control is far more effective
Session A — Upper Body
SES A
Dumbbell Shoulder Press
▾
💡 Shoulder definition makes a real difference in how you look in your wedding outfit. This is one of the best exercises for that.
How to do it
1
Sit or stand with dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing forward
2
Press both dumbbells directly overhead — think of pushing the ceiling away
3
Lower back to shoulder height with control — 2 seconds down
4
Keep your core gently braced — avoid arching your lower back
Common mistakes
✗
Arching your lower back to press the weight — engage your core, keep ribs down
✗
Pressing the weights forward rather than straight up
✗
Shrugging your shoulders as you press — keep them away from your ears
SES A
Dumbbell Lateral Raises
▾
How to do it
1
Stand with dumbbells at your sides, elbows very slightly bent
2
Raise both arms out to the sides, leading with your elbows not your hands
3
Stop when your arms reach shoulder height — no higher
4
Lower slowly and with control — count 3 seconds down
Common mistakes
✗
Raising above shoulder height — it shifts load off the target muscle
✗
Shrugging your shoulders up as you raise — keep them pressed down
✗
Using momentum to swing the weights up — use a lighter weight with strict form
✗
Rushing the lowering phase — the slow return is where toning happens
SES A
Dumbbell Bicep Curl
▾
How to do it
1
Stand tall holding dumbbells at your sides, palms facing forward
2
Keep your upper arms pinned to your sides — they must not move at all
3
Curl both dumbbells towards your shoulders
4
Squeeze for a moment at the top, then lower slowly — 3 seconds down
Common mistakes
✗
Swinging your body to help lift — if this is happening, the weight is too heavy
✗
Moving your upper arms forward — they stay vertical throughout
✗
Dropping the weight fast — the slow lowering builds the shape in the arm
✗
Curling your wrists at the top — keep them straight
SES A
Tricep Overhead Extension
▾
💡 The backs of the upper arms respond very well to this exercise. Consistent work here will show clear results by June.
How to do it
1
Hold one dumbbell with both hands, arms extended straight overhead
2
Keep your elbows close together and pointing forward — not flaring out
3
Lower the dumbbell behind your head by bending only at the elbows
4
Your upper arms stay completely still — only forearms move
5
Press back to full extension, feeling the tricep at the back of the arm contract
Common mistakes
✗
Letting elbows flare wide — keep them close together throughout
✗
Moving the upper arms — they should stay vertical the entire time
✗
Using too heavy a weight — start light and feel the tricep actually working
✗
Arching your lower back — brace gently throughout
Session A — Core (post-partum safe)
SES A
Dead Bug
▾
💡 The dead bug is the gold standard for rebuilding deep core strength after children. It targets the transverse abdominis — the deep muscle that flattens and supports the abdomen — without putting pressure on the pelvic floor or creating the 'doming' effect that sit-ups and crunches can cause.
How to do it
1
Lie on your back, arms pointing straight up toward the ceiling
2
Raise both knees to 90° — shins parallel to the floor, knees over hips
3
Press your lower back firmly into the floor — this must stay down throughout
4
Slowly lower your right arm overhead AND your left leg toward the floor simultaneously
5
Stop before your back arches — bring both back to start, then swap sides
6
That is one rep. Breathe out as you lower, in as you return
Common mistakes
✗
Allowing your lower back to lift off the floor — this defeats the purpose entirely
✗
Moving too fast — this is a slow, controlled, breathing exercise
✗
Holding your breath — focus on exhaling as you extend
✗
Going further than your back can stay flat — range comes with time
SES A
Heel Slides
▾
💡 Heel slides teach the deep core to work while the legs move — exactly the kind of control needed before progressing to harder exercises. If you feel any doming or bulging down the midline of your abdomen during any exercise, stop and go back to this.
How to do it
1
Lie on your back with both knees bent, feet flat on the floor
2
Gently draw your lower abs in — imagine pulling your belly button toward your spine without holding your breath
3
Slowly slide one heel along the floor until your leg is nearly straight
4
Slide it back to the start, maintaining that gentle abdominal tension throughout
5
Alternate legs — 10 slides each side per set
Common mistakes
✗
Letting your lower back arch as you slide out — keep it in contact with the floor
✗
Gripping or tensing hard — the abdominal engagement should be gentle, like 20% effort
✗
Holding your breath — keep breathing normally throughout
✗
Rushing — slow is more effective here
SES A
Bird Dog
▾
💡 Bird dog is excellent for the muscles that run alongside the spine and connect to the pelvis — areas that weaken significantly during pregnancy. It improves posture visibly and quickly.
How to do it
1
Start on all fours — hands under shoulders, knees under hips, back flat
2
Brace your core gently — back should not sag or round
3
Extend your right arm forward and left leg back simultaneously
4
Hold for 2 seconds — both limbs should be roughly parallel to the floor
5
Return slowly to start, then extend left arm and right leg
6
Keep your hips level throughout — don't let them rotate
Common mistakes
✗
Lifting the leg above hip height — parallel to the floor is enough
✗
Letting hips rotate or tilt — keep them square to the floor
✗
Rushing through reps — slow controlled movement with a brief hold is the goal
✗
Letting the lower back sag — if this happens, rest and reset
Session B — Lower Body
SES B
Goblet Squat
▾
💡 Squats are the best exercise for the lower body, full stop. The goblet position makes it easier to keep an upright torso which is particularly good for back health.
How to do it
1
Hold one dumbbell vertically at your chest with both hands
2
Feet shoulder-width apart, toes turned out slightly — wider than feels natural is fine
3
Keeping your chest tall and proud, sit down into the squat
4
Let your elbows track inside your knees — this encourages good depth
5
Drive through your heels to stand, squeezing your glutes at the top
Common mistakes
✗
Knees caving inward — actively push them out over your little toes
✗
Rounding your back to get deeper — chest up is more important than depth
✗
Heels coming off the floor — if this happens, widen your stance slightly
✗
Not going deep enough — at least until thighs are parallel to floor
SES B
Romanian Deadlift
▾
💡 Excellent for posture and the backs of the legs. Also strengthens the muscles alongside the spine — particularly beneficial after pregnancy.
How to do it
1
Stand holding dumbbells at your thighs, palms facing your legs
2
Keep a soft bend in your knees throughout — this is not a straight-leg exercise
3
Push your hips back behind you as if closing a door with your bottom
4
The dumbbells slide down your thighs toward your shins — keep them close
5
Stop when you feel a strong stretch in your hamstrings — drive hips forward to stand
Common mistakes
✗
Rounding your back — the most important thing to get right
✗
Squatting the weight down rather than hinging — hips move back, not down
✗
Letting the dumbbells swing away from your legs
✗
Going lower than your flexibility allows — the stretch is the signal to stop
SES B
Dumbbell Reverse Lunge
▾
💡 Reverse lunges are gentler on the knees than forward lunges and are safer for the pelvic floor. They target the glutes and front of the thigh very effectively.
How to do it
1
Stand tall with dumbbells at your sides
2
Step one foot back — further back than feels natural, landing on the ball of the foot
3
Lower your back knee toward the floor without letting it crash down
4
Your front shin should stay vertical — knee sits directly over the ankle
5
Push through the front heel to return, then alternate legs
Common mistakes
✗
Front knee drifting past your toes — if this happens, step back further
✗
Leaning forward — keep your torso upright throughout
✗
Taking too short a step — this strains the front knee
✗
Letting the back knee slam the floor — controlled lowering
SES B
Glute Bridge
▾
💡 The glute bridge is one of the best exercises for rebuilding both glute strength and pelvic floor function after having children. Adding a dumbbell across your hips increases the challenge as you get stronger.
How to do it
1
Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor hip-width apart
2
Before lifting, gently engage your pelvic floor — a light 'lift and squeeze'
3
Drive your hips up by squeezing your glutes firmly
4
At the top, your body forms a diagonal line from knees to shoulders
5
Hold the top position for 2 full seconds, squeezing hard
6
Lower slowly — 3 seconds down
Common mistakes
✗
Using your lower back to push up rather than your glutes — feel the squeeze in your bottom
✗
Feet too close or too far from your body — at the top your shins should be vertical
✗
Rushing through reps — slow controlled reps with the hold are far more effective
✗
Forgetting the pelvic floor engagement — this small step makes the exercise much more valuable
Session B — Core (post-partum safe)
SES B
Dead Bug
▾
💡 The dead bug appears in both sessions as it is the foundational core exercise for this programme. Building this pattern will carry over into every other exercise.
How to do it
1
Lie on your back, arms pointing straight up
2
Raise both knees to 90° — shins parallel to the floor
3
Press your lower back firmly into the floor — non-negotiable
4
Slowly extend right arm overhead and left leg toward the floor
5
Stop before your back arches — return slowly and swap sides
6
Breathe out as you extend, in as you return
Common mistakes
✗
Lower back lifting off the floor — stop if this happens
✗
Moving too quickly — this is a breathing, control exercise
✗
Holding your breath — exhale on the extension
✗
Overreaching range — a small, controlled range done well beats large range done badly
Cardio — Elliptical
CARDIO
Elliptical Trainer
▾
How to do it
1
Step on with feet flat on the pedals
2
Stand tall — don't lean on the machine
3
Hold the handles lightly — work comes from the legs
4
Maintain a steady pace where you can just about hold a conversation
5
Push through the full stride — don't shorten your range
Common mistakes
✗
Leaning heavily on the handles
✗
Hunching forward
✗
Too little resistance — add enough that you have to push
✗
Too short a stride — use the full range of the pedal
Session C — Whole Body (from Week 5)
SES C
Goblet Squat
▾
How to do it
1
Hold one dumbbell vertically at your chest, cupped in both hands
2
Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out
3
Sit back and down — thighs parallel or as low as comfortable
4
Drive through heels to stand, squeeze glutes at the top
Common mistakes
✗
Letting knees cave inward — push them out over your toes
✗
Rounding the upper back — the dumbbell at chest helps prevent this
✗
Rising onto your toes — keep heels flat throughout
The dumbbell position at chest naturally encourages upright posture — a real advantage. This is the most complete lower body exercise you can do.

Romanian Deadlift
▾

1
Hold dumbbells at thigh height, slight knee bend throughout
2
Hinge at the hips — push them back behind you
3
Lower until you feel the stretch in your hamstrings
4
Drive hips forward to stand, squeeze glutes
Common mistakes
✗
Rounding the lower back — stop before this happens
✗
Bending the knees too much — this becomes a squat
✗
Letting dumbbells swing away from your legs — keep them close
Excellent for posture and the backs of the legs. Also strengthens the muscles alongside the spine — particularly beneficial after pregnancy.
SES C
Dumbbell Shoulder Press
▾
How to do it
1
Seated, dumbbells at shoulder height, palms forward
2
Sit tall — do not lean back
3
Press straight overhead to full extension
4
Lower slowly — 2 full seconds down
Common mistakes
✗
Arching the lower back — sit tall and engage core
✗
Pressing forward instead of straight up
✗
Dropping the weights quickly on the way down — control matters
Shoulder definition makes a real difference in how you look in your wedding outfit. Consistent work here will show clear results by June.
SES C
Dumbbell Row
▾
How to do it
1
Hand and same-side knee on a bench, or stand bent with hand on thigh
2
Dumbbell hanging in free hand, arm straight
3
Drive elbow up and back — think elbow to ceiling
4
Squeeze shoulder blade at the top
5
Lower slowly under control
Common mistakes
✗
Rotating the torso to lift — keep hips square
✗
Using momentum — slow and deliberate
✗
Letting the dumbbell drop quickly — resist the lowering
Strengthens the back and improves posture. Counteracts the rounding that comes from desk work and carrying children. One of the best things you can do for your back.
SES C
Reverse Lunge
▾

1
Stand tall, dumbbells at your sides
2
Step one foot back, lower back knee toward the floor
3
Front knee stays over the ankle — not past the toes
4
Push through the front heel to return
5
Alternate legs each rep

Rushing the movement — control the descent
Reverse lunges are gentler on the knees than forward lunges and safer for the pelvic floor. Adding dumbbells makes this a genuinely demanding whole-body movement.
SES C
Glute Bridge
▾
How to do it
1

2
Gently engage your pelvic floor before lifting
3
Drive hips upward, squeezing glutes hard at the top
4
Hold for 2 seconds at the top
5
Lower slowly — do not let hips drop quickly

✗

One of the best exercises for both glute strength and pelvic floor function. No weight needed — bodyweight alone is highly effective when done slowly.
SES C
Dead Bug
▾
How to do it
1
Lie on your back, arms pointing up toward the ceiling
2
Bend knees to 90° — shins parallel to the floor
3
Exhale and slowly extend opposite arm and leg toward the floor
4
Keep your lower back pressed flat against the floor throughout
5
Return slowly, then repeat on the other side
Common mistakes
✗
Allowing your lower back to lift off the floor — if it does, reduce range
✗
Holding your breath — exhale on the extension
✗
Moving too quickly — slow is the point
The gold standard for rebuilding deep core strength. Targets the transverse abdominis without pressure on the pelvic floor. The finisher for Session C.
